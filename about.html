Overview

    Ntare School is a prestigious, government-founded boarding 
    school for boys located in Mbarara, Uganda, established in 1956
             by Scottish educator William Crichton. Known for elite academic 
                performance and sports excellence, it has produced prominent leaders,
                    including the presidents of Uganda and Rwanda. The school is renowned 
    for fostering a diverse,competitive, and "liberal"
    environment.

Historical Background

Foundation: Established in 1956 on Kamukuzi Hill in Mbarara, it was one of the first three government-founded secondary schools in Uganda that was not initially founded by missionaries.

Founder: William Crichton, a Scottish educator, served as the first headteacher and established a legacy of academic excellence.

Origins The school started with 60 students using facilities from a former government teacher training college.

Name Origin: Named after the Omugabe (King) Ntare V of Nkore, honoring local history.
