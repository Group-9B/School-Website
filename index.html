School History

Ntare School was founded in 1956
by the Congregation of the
Holy Ghost Fathers in Mbarara,
western Uganda. The school
established to provide quality
education while developing strong
moral values and leadership skills
among young men
Over the years, Ntare School has
grown into one of Uganda’s leading
secondary schools, known for its
academic excellence, discipline,
and leadership training. It has
built a strong reputation as a
center for nurturing responsible
and capable citizens
The school has produced many
influential leaders,
Yoweri Museveni and Paul Kagame
which has earned it recognition
as a “school of leaders” in East Africa
Today, Ntare School continues to uphold
its tradition of excellence, focusing on
shaping students into disciplined,
knowledgeable, and responsible
ready to contribute to society
